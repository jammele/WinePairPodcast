# FAQ / Q&A Format Rules

For each podcast episode page, implement TWO distinct sections. Do NOT combine them.

---

## Section 1: Key Questions We Answer (Top Teaser)

**Placement:** Immediately after the intro/summary paragraph, before the main show notes.

**Purpose:** Signal to search engines and AI systems what questions the page answers. Improve AEO visibility. Quick preview for readers.

**Rules:**
- Title: "Key Questions We Answer"
- 3-5 questions MAX
- Questions only -- no answers
- Do not expand into a full FAQ
- Simple bullet list formatting

**Writing style:**
- Natural, conversational (how a real person would search)
- Short, specific, clear
- No jargon unless necessary
- No keyword stuffing

**Content focus:**
- Buying decisions ("Is this wine worth it?")
- Misconceptions ("Is Riesling always sweet?")
- Definitions ("What does petrol mean in wine?")
- Pairing/use cases ("What food goes with this?")

**Example:**

Key Questions We Answer
- Is German Riesling always sweet?
- What does petrol smell mean in wine?
- Is German Riesling a good value wine?
- What foods pair well with Riesling?

---

## Section 2: Full Q&A (Bottom Section)

**Placement:** Near the bottom of the page, after show notes, ratings, and tasting notes. Before or after transcript (keep consistent).

**Purpose:** Structured, answer-rich content for SEO and AEO. Capture long-tail queries. Support featured snippets and AI answer extraction.

**Rules:**
- Title: "KEY INSIGHTS & FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS"
- 4-8 question-and-answer pairs (aim for 4-5)
- Format: "Q:" before each question, "A:" before each answer, both on separate lines
- Questions and Q/A labels in bold
- Each answer immediately follows its question

**Answer guidelines:**
- 2-4 sentences per answer
- Front-load the answer -- be direct in the first sentence
- Natural, conversational tone consistent with podcast voice
- No fluff, filler, or overly formal language
- Be confident and clear -- do not hedge excessively

**Content alignment:**
- Questions should align with the "Key Questions" section (can overlap, no need to match exactly)
- Expand beyond the teaser with clarifications, nuance, and context from tasting/review

**Tone:**
- Informative, slightly irreverent, anti-snob
- Plainspoken and accessible
- No em dashes
- No cliches or generic wine language

**SEO/AEO:**
- Natural phrasing that matches real search queries
- Include relevant keywords organically
- Answers should be able to stand alone if pulled into search or AI responses

**Example:**

Q&A

Is German Riesling always sweet?
No. German Riesling ranges from bone dry to very sweet. Many bottles labeled "trocken" are completely dry, while others can have noticeable residual sugar.

What does petrol mean in Riesling?
"Petrol" refers to a specific aroma that can develop in Riesling as it ages. It may sound off-putting, but in balance it is considered a classic and desirable characteristic.

---

## Important Constraints

- Do NOT merge the two sections
- Do NOT put full answers at the top of the page
- Do NOT remove or relocate the bottom Q&A section
- Do NOT exceed question limits (top: 3-5, bottom: 4-8)
- Aim for the lower end of the Q&A range (4-5 pairs is usually right; 7-8 is too many)
- Maintain a clean, readable page layout

## Common mistakes to avoid

- **Em dashes:** "--" counts as an em dash. Do not use it anywhere in either section.
- **Generic wine language:** Avoid phrases like "earthy undertones," "bright and red-fruited," "good acidity," "red-fruit notes," "silky tannins." Describe what the wine actually tastes like in plain terms.
- **Hedging instead of front-loading:** Do not start answers with "It depends..." or similar. Lead with the direct answer, then add nuance.
- **Neutral/editorial tone:** Answers should feel like Joe and Carmela talking, not a wine guide. Slightly irreverent, plainspoken, anti-snob.
